Clear your mind and get refreshed in the great outdoors.

Columbus Parks

Columbus offers multiple parks where you can enjoy the fresh air, let the kids play, or take a hike. Some of our parks have tennis and basketball courts as well as baseball, football and soccer fields; were you can have a picnic or enjoy some outdoor sports.

Columbus Fall line Trace offers over 10 miles of walking, running, or biking trail that extends through out the city. If mountain biking is more your style, visit Standing Boy Trail or Flat Rock Park for some off-road action. No bike, no problem, we have a few bike shops offering affordable rentals for the whole family.

Explore

Eplore space and our universe at the Coca-Cola Space and Science Center, sponsored by Columbus state University. Your family can enjoy a sky tour and planetarium show in the Omnisphere Theater, or test your piloting skills on our interactive exhibits and so much more!

Smell the roses, learn about horticulture, or enjoy a colorful setting for family photos at Columbus Botanicle Gardens. It is located on 36 acres of land in north Columbus, GA. The Gardens consist of 4 developed acres and a beatiful walking trail throughout the remaining landscape.

If you are more interested in reptiles, amphibians and creepy crawlies, come visit Oxbow Meadows sponsored by Columbus State University. Here your family will get up close and personal with nature, and participate in hands on activites and learning facilitated by knowledgable instructors. Each organization hosts seasonal events and activites for children and adults of all ages. suggested $5.00 donation for entry to both of these amazing places.
