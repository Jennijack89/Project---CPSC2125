-Columbus has rich history and culture enriched by the arts.

History & Art

Our museums connect our community with it's history and they provide unique and seasonal programs for people of all interests and all ages.

Come visit The Columbus Museum, National Civil War Naval Museum and The National Infantry Museum and Soilder Center , where American art and history come to life and you can learn about our local and military history.


Theater & Music

Theater and music are the twin heart beats of our community. On any given night you can find a place to listen to live music, dance, and/or attend a play.

Visit The Springer Opera House or The RiverCenter located in uptown Columbus Georgia. We are also the home to Columbus State University's renowned Schwob School of Music, which provide's musical and instrumental instruction for students of all ages, beginner to advanced.


Our Libraries

Attend It

The Chattahoochie Valley Libraries offer a treasure trove of resources that you can gain access to with a libray card. They have a wide variety of programs for children, teens, and adults weekly.

Check It Out

Not only do they have a wide variety of books for you to check out. You can check out musical instruments, steam kits, telescopes, microscopes, video recording hardware and more.

Research It

They offer resources to help you research anything from genealogy, business, history, languages, health and wellness and more.

Download It

In additon, they offer free sofwtare for you to download music, magazines, ebooks, and audiobooks.


Artistic Recreation

Although we have many outlets for the professionally trained artists, we also have plenty of places where a novice can get in touch with their artistic side. You can vist our No Shame theater and put your talents on display or try your hand at imprompt acting.You can attend a pottery class at Britt David or Bare Ware Pottery, or head over to brushes and beverages to have a paint session with your friends. Whatever your interest Columbus have something for everyone.
